MALAYSIA TOURISM STATISTICS (2016-2023)

In recent years, Malaysia's tourism sector has experienced remarkable growth, driven by its variety of attractions, rich cultural heritage, and strategic location. To better understand the industry's performace and its overall impact during this period, it is crucial to analyze key performance indicators.

From 2016 to 2023, Singapore was the top country contributing to tourist arrivals with over 61 million visitors, followed by Indonesia, Thailand, China and Brunei.

Malaysia's tourist arrivals, peaking between 2016 and 2019, with totals exceeding 25 millions tourists annually. Following the relaxation of global travel restrictions in 2022, Malaysia witnessed a significant rebound in tourism, and the positive trend continued into 2023.

Shopping consistently remained the largest expenditure category, peaking in 2019 at RM28,944.2 (33.6% of total expenditure). It saw a dip in 2021 due to the pandemic.

In 2023, Kuala Lumpur, the capital city of Malaysia, leads with 18.4 million arrivals due to its status as a business and tourist center, drawing visitors for its iconic landmarks, shopping and international events. Pulau Pinang with over 7.2 million in 2023 arrivals is also a major tourism hub due to its cultural heritage, beaches and UNESCO-listed George Town. This makes it a popular destination despite having a smaller area compared to other states.

üöó Mode Of Transport

In 2023, land transportation was the dominant mode, accounting for 55.8% of arrivals, with over 11 million tourists using this method. This reflects the convenience and accessibility of land routes, especially for regional travelers. Air travel followed with 36.7% of arrivals, highlighting the importance of international and long-distance travel for tourism, with over 7.3 million tourists arriving by air. Tourists have strong preference for land and air travel.
